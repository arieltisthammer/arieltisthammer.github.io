My name is Ariel Tisthammer, and I’m currently a fourth-year psychology student at the University of California, Riverside. I have a GPA of 3.99, which demonstrates my dedication and passion for understanding the brain. As I am about to graduate soon, I am excited to transition from a class setting to a more hands-on learning experience. Particularly, I aim to do this through internships and lab work that will allow me to explore my specific interest in neuroscience. Throughout my academic journey, I have engaged in the various areas of psychology, such as brain psychology, social psychology, personality psychology, child development psychology, and more. This gives me a solid foundation to do well post-graduation, as it equips me with a more multifaceted perspective on psychology as a whole.

In the summer of 2023, I took on a position at Tan Cha, a boba shop, where I gained valuable skills and knowledge beyond the lecture room. This experience taught me essential skills such as time management and efficiency, which are crucial in any fast-paced environment. Additionally, working in customer service enhanced my ability to communicate effectively and empathetically with diverse individuals. Overall, I learned the importance of adaptability, teamwork, and maintaining a positive attitude, all of which I believe will serve me well in my future in psychology, as I must be understanding and empathetic to participants.

Outside of academia and my work experience, I like to spend my time hiking. Hiking provides me a time to reflect and return to a calmer and more patient version of myself. Additionally, I heavily enjoy reading as it introduces me to new ideas that I can apply to my field of study. Ultimately, I am determined to expand my knowledge and experiences within psychology and neuroscience. I am confident that my academic background, combined with my work experience and personal interests, will allow me to contribute meaningfully to any lab team I join. I hope to make a positive impact on everyone I work with.
